Online Safety

In 2022, a national survey conducted by the eSafety Commissioner asked Australian adults aged 18 to 65 years about their online experiences. The survey found that 75% had at least one negative online experience in the last 12 months, an increase from 58% in 2019.

Personal Fraud (2023-24)

1 in 10 Australians experienced card fraud in 2023-24. Card fraud increased from 8.7% in 2022-23 to 9.9% in 2023-24. Scam victimisation increased from 2.5% in 2022-23 to 3.1% in 2023-24

Source: ABS (Graph taken from source)

Personal Fraud (over the last decade)

In the 12 months prior to interview in 2014-15, an estimated 1.6 million Australians experienced personal fraud, or 8.5% of the population aged 15 and over. This is an increase from the proportion of persons who experienced personal fraud in 2010-11 (6.7%).
