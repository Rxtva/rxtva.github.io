Online Safety

In 2022, a national survey conducted by the eSafety Commissioner asked Australian adults aged 18 to 65 years about their online experiences. The survey found:
• 75% had at least one negative online experience in the last 12 months, an increase from 58% in 2019
• 32% were sent unwanted inappropriate content (up from 14% in 2019)
• 30% were called offensive names (up from 12% in 2019)
• 25% had personal information used without consent (up from 11% in 2019)
• 24% had things said to them to offend, distress or harm (up from 11% in 2019).

Source: ABS (Graph taken from source)

Personal Fraud (2023-24)

Personal fraud includes card fraud, identity theft, and scams (phishing, romance, computer support, financial advice and more)
In 2023-24, an estimated 14% of persons aged 15 years and over experienced one or more types of personal fraud, including:
• 9.9% (2.1 million) who experienced card fraud
• 3.1% (675,300) who experienced a scam
• 1.2% (255,100) who experienced identity theft
• 2.0% (433,000) who experienced online impersonation

Source: ABS (Graph taken from source)

Personal Fraud (over the last decade)

This data includes information about persons who experienced selected personal fraud offences, including card fraud, identity theft and a range of personal scams.
2007:
• 2.4% of people aged 15+ experienced card fraud.
• 0.8% of people aged 15+ experienced identity-theft.
• 2.0% of people aged 15+ responded to a scam.
2010-11:
• 3.7% of people aged 15+ experienced card fraud.
• 2.9% of people aged 15+ responded to a scam.
2014-15:
• 5.9% of people aged 15+ experienced card fraud.
• 0.7% of people aged 15+ experienced identity-theft.
• 2.4% of people aged 15+ responded to a scam.

Source: ABS (Graph taken from source)
