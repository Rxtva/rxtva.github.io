Phishing

Scams that trick you into giving out personal info.
• Phishing attacks use fake communications that seem legitimate.
• Victims may click malicious links, download infected files, or give personal data.
• It is a common and sophisticated form of cybercrime.
• Spear phishing targets specific individuals with personalized messages.
• Other types include vishing (voice), smishing (SMS), and quishing (QR codes).

Malware

Malicious software that harms your computer or steals data.
• Malware includes viruses, adware, spyware, and ransomware.
• Cybercriminals use it to gather valuable data for financial gain, such as banking details and personal information.
• It can infect various devices like phones, PCs, and gaming systems.
• Malware spreads by hiding in data files and executing when the file runs.

Learn more

Hacking

Exploiting system vulnerabilities to gain unauthorized access. It is maliciously used for data theft or damage.
• Hackers use methods like social engineering, phishing, malware, and software vulnerabilities.
• Reasons for hacking range from stealing sensitive data for profit to political motives or activism.
• Hacking involves serious security risks and motivations can differ greatly among hackers.

Learn more

Identity Theft

Someone pretending to be you using stolen personal details.
• Involves stealing details like names, Social Security numbers, or credit card information.
• Can lead to new accounts being opened, unauthorized transactions, or crimes committed with the victim's identity.
• Consequences include damaged credit and wrongful arrest.

Learn more

Cyberbullying

Using digital platforms to harass or bully others.
• It occurs via SMS, texts, apps, social media, forums, or gaming platforms.
• Involves sharing negative, harmful, false, or mean content about someone.
• Can include sharing private information that causes embarrassment.
• Examples include spreading lies, posting embarrassing content, sending abusive messages, impersonating others, and engaging in harassment using AI tools.

Learn more

Ransomware

Locking your files and demanding payment to release them.
• It spreads through phishing emails, malicious ads, social engineering, and exploit kits.
• Modern attacks may also threaten to leak sensitive data, increasing pressure on victims.
• Ransomware poses a major risk to all organizations, using complex extortion tactics.
