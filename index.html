Cybercrime Stats

The internet’s awesome for staying connected, but it also comes with risks—especially for teens. Scams, sketchy links, and even hackers can pop up when you least expect it. A lot of cybercrime these days happens through social media or games, where people pretend to be someone they’re not. Believe it or not, almost 40% of young people have faced stuff like online bullying, scams, or even identity theft. Knowing what’s out there helps you stay smart and avoid getting caught up in it. Threat Awareness and Help

Not everything online is as chill as it looks. Some messages, links, or even friend requests can be straight-up dodgy. Phishing, online creeps, cyberbullying—it’s all real, but the good news is there are ways to stay safe. If something feels off, it probably is. Talk to someone you trust, use tools like the eSafety website, or call a helpline. You don’t have to deal with it alone—just stay aware, trust your gut, and speak up when something seems wrong. Learn how to spot dangers online and where to get help. Staying informed is the first step to staying safe.
